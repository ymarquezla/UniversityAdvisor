The second reason is Valencia itself. The city has a rapidly growing tech ecosystem. It's young, international, and energetic. And as a Spanish speaker, I won't just be studying here — I'll be integrating. Living, working, connecting. That matters to me.

The third reason is my language advantage. With C1 Spanish, I can engage fully in courses taught in Spanish. I can work on group projects without a language gap. I can interview with Spanish companies. Most international applicants don't have that edge — I do, and I intend to use it.

The fourth reason is UPV's reputation. As one of Spain's top technical universities, a degree from UPV carries weight across Europe and internationally. That reputation combined with the SEAMIC program's specific focus is a powerful combination.

I didn't choose SEAMIC because it was the easy option. I chose it because when I researched programs across Europe, this one kept rising to the top. Valencia isn't just where I want to study. It's where I want to begin building my career."
