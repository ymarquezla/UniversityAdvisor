- **Eligibility**: Rising sophomores, juniors, seniors planning PhD
- **Fields**: Biomedical sciences, physical sciences, engineering, social sciences, humanities

- **Compensation**: Funded summer research position

### 2. **Bruno Signature Summer Internship Program (SPRINT)**
- **What**: Internships with Brown alumni or affiliated organizations
- **Goal**: Make high-quality internships accessible to all students
- **Application**: Up to 5 SPRINT opportunities
- **Deadline**: February 2, 2026 (12pm noon)

- **Note**: May be open to non-Brown students in some cases (verify)

- **Why Apply**: Direct AI/Data Science experience, Brown faculty collaboration

## COMPENSATION COMPARISON

### Rhode Island Opportunities
- **SEACORP**: $20-$30/hour estimated ($6,400-$9,600 for 10 weeks)
- **CVS Health**: $22-$35/hour estimated ($7,040-$11,200 for 10 weeks)
- **Citizens Bank**: $20-$30/hour estimated
- **Startups**: $18-$28/hour typical
- **Housing Cost**: $0 (live at home) ← BIG ADVANTAGE

**Total Summer Earnings (RI)**: $6,400-$11,200 + $0 housing = **Maximum savings**

### Massachusetts Opportunities
- **MIT Lincoln Lab**: $25-$35/hour + housing support ($8,000-$11,200 + housing)
- **CHIP AI**: Research stipend (varies, ~$5,000-$8,000 total)
- **Tech Companies**: $22-$40/hour ($7,040-$12,800)
- **Housing Cost**: -$2,000-$4,000 (if not provided) ← Reduces net earnings

**Total Summer Earnings (MA)**: $8,000-$12,800 - $2,000-$4,000 housing = **$4,000-$10,800 net**

### ROI Analysis

**Best Financial Outcome**: RI internship at CVS Health or SEACORP
- Earn $7,000-$11,000
- No housing costs
- Live at home
- **Net savings: $7,000-$11,000**

**Best Experience Outcome**: MIT Lincoln Lab AI internship
- Earn $8,000-$11,000
- Housing provided/supported
- World-class AI research
- MIT affiliation
- **Net savings: $6,000-$9,000** (after minimal housing costs)

**Balanced Approach**: Apply to both RI and MA opportunities
- Priority: RI companies (financial + local advantage)
- Reach: MIT Lincoln Lab, CHIP (prestige + experience)
- Backup: Other Boston companies, Brown research
